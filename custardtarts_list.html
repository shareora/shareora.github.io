Ingredients

Pastry

  • Flour
    360g
  • Shortening
    185g
  • Hot Water
    74ml
  • Salt
    0.5g

Custard

  • Eggs
    3
  • Sugar
    100g
  • Milk
    355ml
  • Yellow colouring
    few drops

Instructions

  1. 1

    Sift the flour and salt into a large mixing bowl. Add the shortening and rub it into the flour using your fingertips until the mixture resembles fine breadcrumbs.

  2. 2

    Gradually add hot water to the mixture, stirring until a firm dough forms. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead gently until smooth.

  3. 3

    Roll out the dough to a 3mm thickness. Using an 8cm fluted cutter, cut out rounds and gently press them into greased patty tins.

  4. 4

    In a mixing bowl, beat together the eggs and sugar until well combined. Gradually add the milk, then stir in the food colouring and mix thoroughly. Carefully pour the custard mixture into the prepared pastry cases.

  5. 5

    Bake in a preheated hot oven (230C) for 10 minutes. Then reduce the heat to moderately hot (200C) and continue baking for another 10â€“15 minutes, or until the custard is just set.

Recipe Info

Prep Time: 70 mins
Total Time: 2 hours
Servings: 30
Category: Baking, Desserts
Cuisine Chinese
Difficulty: Easy

Recipe Notes

Storage: Refrigerate in an airtight container Seperate layer with parchment or wax paper if stacking is necessary. Store in the fridge for up to 3 days. Avoid leaving at room temperature. Due to the dairy and eggs in the custard not safe to leave it out more than 2 hours.

Variations: Add fruits such as strawberries, blueberries or any others.

Tip: Always allow the custard tarts to colo to room temperature before storing to prevent condensation, which can make the pastry soggy.
